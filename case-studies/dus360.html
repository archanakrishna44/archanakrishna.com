Fannie Mae

DUS 360: Scaling a Legacy Loan Platform for Record-Breaking Demand

Impact
9 UX solutions shipped pre-launch · 24 improvement recommendations
Timeline
4 months
Role
Lead Design Strategist

When Fannie Mae forecasted loan forbearance volumes that would exceed all previous records, I led the UX transformation of their DUS 360 platform. The existing system, built after the 2009 financial crisis, relied on manual processes that couldn't scale. Without rapid transformation, the platform would become a bottleneck impacting Fannie Mae's ability to manage their multifamily loan portfolio.

My Strategy

Ruthless prioritization: I led a workshop with product leadership to identify the highest-impact flows for research and design to focus on.
Rapid low-fidelity prototyping: We mapped low-fidelity wireframes directly to design system components to compress handoff.
Research advocacy: I joined after development had already started with no discovery time built in, so I pushed for pre-launch testing to capture holistic user feedback and validation.

Research & Results

That advocacy paid off. I combined usability testing with discovery questions, getting validation data and foundational user insights in one effort. Tested 18 participants across 2 internal groups and 5 external servicers revealing 24 recommendations for improvement across 5 pain point themes.

We shipped 9 high-impact UX solutions before launch. Success in providing effective research and design solutions led to requests for continued partnership to implement the remaining 15 recommendations across the next two program increments.

The Real Victory

The real victory was the organizational shift. A product team that asked for UI quick fixes evolved to advocating for user research. I transformed a reactive 2-day turnaround culture into proactive sprint-ahead design, mentored 2 designers, and established lasting UX partnerships across Multifamily.
