Fannie Mae

Financial Forecasting Platform: From Scattered Complaints to Shared Platform Strategy

Impact
Shifted investment conversation to unified platform strategy
Timeline
6 weeks
Role
Lead Design Strategist

Four analytics teams at Fannie Mae used the financial forecasting platform daily. All four had complaints. All four had submitted separate investment requests. Leadership saw four small problems competing for funding, but no one had mapped their processes side-by-side to see whether these were unique issues or symptoms of something systemic.

The Approach

I led a six-week service design initiative to answer that question. The challenge: conducting research across 4 teams and 6 role types in a timeline that would normally take months. I designed a two-phase methodology, meeting with Product Owners first to build baseline blueprints, then bringing in role-specific participants to validate and enrich. This cut session time in half and demonstrated respect for stakeholders' expertise before asking for their time.

The Synthesis

The synthesis revealed what no one had seen. Forty-nine pain points collapsed into six shared themes. Four teams weren't asking for four different things. They were all experiencing the same systemic failures.

Among those themes, I identified a critical cross-cutting pattern: audit-related issues creating process vulnerability during regulatory review. This needed to be elevated to leadership because anything affecting audit readiness carries regulatory weight.

The Result

I delivered prioritized recommendations using an importance-difficulty matrix, with issues spanning multiple teams weighted for near-term implementation. Leadership adopted this framework for their roadmap.

The investment conversation shifted from debating which team's needs to prioritize to discussing how to fix the platform.
