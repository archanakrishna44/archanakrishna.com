Fannie Mae

HomePath.com: From Support Crisis to Strategic Innovation

Impact
$720K–1.8M projected savings
Timeline
4-week sprint
Role
Lead Design Strategist

Fannie Mae faced a 300% increase in customer service costs (from $5 to $15 per interaction) while HomePath.com was driving 80,000 annual REO-related support calls. Strategic heuristic evaluation revealed why users were calling instead of self-serving: average usability scores of only 1.39 out of 3.0, with real estate professionals scoring even lower at 1.27. The platform lacked any usability baseline and threatened $1.2M in annual support costs.

Strategic Approach

Chose heuristic evaluations over user research due to 4-week timeline constraints, delivering quantifiable insights while establishing HomePath's first usability baseline across both user personas and three platforms
Identified four high-impact focus areas: homepage wayfinding, search functionality, offer workflows, and FAQ consolidation
Developed 53 targeted solutions designed to achieve five key experience improvements: streamlined decision-making, optimized flows, reduced task time, faster information access, and clearer user expectations
Strategically mapped solutions to call drivers and organized into implementation phases for maximum adoption

Results & Impact

Created a transformation roadmap projected to save $720K-1.8M over three years through 20-50% call volume reduction. Beyond financial impact, stakeholders saw such significant value that they requested dedicated continued partnership to explore and execute on all recommendations we provided, establishing lasting cross-functional relationships and proving that strategic UX can deliver enterprise-scale impact without massive research investments.
